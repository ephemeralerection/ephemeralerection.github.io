Description

First Planet Earth series was a landmark point in documentary film-making, and got all the recognition it warranted. The second installation of the series follows suit, and raises the bar some more. Every aspect of Planet Earth II pushes the boundaries of what nature documentary can be; the cinematography is second to none, the narration is perfectly timed and tied to the scenes, the music is brilliant, and the scenes are unimaginably meticulously planned.

It is extremely difficult to elevate one aspect of this documentary above the others because everything is so painstakingly melded in a seamless amalgam of audio-visual splendor. But, if I had to pick one, it would be the cinematography. The technological advances and incredible creativity of the production crew made for an unprecedented and intimate view in the animal life. The unobtrusiveness and quality of camera work in the show is so unbelievable I had to convince one of my friends it is not CGI. After a few minutes of watching, you get drawn in and feel a part of the scene. A remote observer, but a part of it nonetheless.

What Planet Earth 2 so masterfully succeeds in, is forming a sense of oneness and newfound compassion towards flora and fauna urban estrangement has taken away from us. Even if you somehow manage to resist the aforementioned feelings (you psychopath!), the cinematic quality itself is worthy of revering. What this show managed to do for me personally is, entice remorse for torrenting the show. And that is quite a feat!

Description

Incredibly gripping, morally complex, suspenseful, and consistently involving drama that studies family relationships under internal and external stress. A Separation deals with a middle-class Iranian family that is torn between staying in Iran or going abroad so their child can have a better life. Already volatile situation is exacerbated by a collision with a different family and all their issues. The way Farhadi manages to interweave personality traits, societal paradigms and ethical issues in such a consistent and theatrical form is nothing short of breathtaking.

Beautiful writing and astonishing acting combined with masterful Farhadi's directing makes this feel like a documentary filmed with an invisible camera. A masterclass in psychological filmmaking.
